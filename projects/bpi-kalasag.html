2nd Place — BPI Data Wave Hackathon

BPI Kalasag

A proactive digital shield designed for
senior financial wellness.

Role Team Lead · PM · Researcher · Developer
Duration 6 Weeks
Track Hyper-Personalization

The Problem

4 million Filipino seniors face a growing "confidence gap" in digital banking. As financial services move online, elderly users are increasingly left behind — vulnerable to fraud, overwhelmed by complex interfaces, and disconnected from the protection they deserve.

This isn't just a usability problem. It's a trust problem, a safety problem, and ultimately, a dignity problem.

The Approach

Over 6 rigorous weeks, I led a multidisciplinary team through research, ideation, prototyping, and presentation — wearing the hats of Team Leader, Product Manager, Researcher, and Developer simultaneously.

We designed BPI Kalasag around three pillars:

  • Radically Simple UI — Stripped-down interfaces with large text, clear iconography, and guided flows designed specifically for senior cognition patterns
  • Collaborative Guardian System — Family members receive real-time alerts and can co-manage accounts, bridging the trust gap between seniors and digital banking
  • Proactive AI Defense — Intelligent transaction flagging that catches suspicious activity before it becomes fraud, protecting seniors without requiring tech literacy

Impact & Results

  • Secured 2nd Place in the BPI Data Wave Hackathon's Hyper-Personalization track
  • Addressed the confidence gap for 4 million seniors with a comprehensive solution
  • Demonstrated cross-functional leadership — research, design, engineering, and strategy — in a high-stakes competitive environment
  • Validated the approach with judges from BPI's leadership team

Reflection

Kalasag means "shield" in Filipino. That's what this project was about — not just building software, but building protection. The most meaningful insight from this experience was that inclusive design isn't about simplifying for the "less capable." It's about recognizing that the system was never designed for them in the first place — and choosing to change that.
